Presentation

This international conference focuses on the interplay between modular forms, geometry, complex analysis, dynamical systems, and combinatorics. Modular forms encode deep arithmetic information through L-functions and Galois representations. Complex analytic methods (Bergman spaces, growth estimates, analytic continuation) investigate hyperbolicity in complex geometry. Dynamics such as Perron numbers and β‑expansions illustrate how symbolic dynamics encodes arithmetic information. Special functions (theta functions, hypergeometric series, orthogonal polynomials) and combinatorics serve as a bridge linking these areas.

Connections with other fields: Orthogonal polynomials and special functions are widely used in numerical analysis, approximation theory, signal processing, and control theory. Combinatorics and dynamical systems have applications in coding theory and data compression. Complex analysis techniques appear in quantum mechanics and image reconstruction. This conference aims to foster exchanges between pure and applied mathematicians.

Detailed Topics

  • Modular forms, quasimodular forms, Hecke groups, and Zagier-type conjectures
  • Non-Kähler hyperbolicity for compact complex manifolds
  • Bergman spaces, complex analysis, and their applications in quantum mechanics and image reconstruction
  • Perron numbers, β‑expansions, and applications in data compression
  • Combinatorics and its interactions with special functions and arithmetic applications
  • Limit sets of monotone maps on regular curves. Recurrence and nonwandering sets of local dendrite maps
  • d‑orthogonality, orthogonal polynomials, and the q‑Askey scheme
  • Special functions and their applications in numerical analysis, signal processing, and control theory
