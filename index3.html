Presentation

This international conference focuses on the interplay between modular forms, geometry, complex analysis, and dynamical systems. Modular forms, with their Fourier expansions and transformation laws, remain central to modern number theory, encoding deep information related to L-functions and Galois representations. Key themes include the use of complex analytic methods—such as the study of Bergman spaces, growth estimates, and analytic continuation, to investigate hyperbolicity in complex geometry. The program also explores connections between dynamics, such as Perron numbers and β‑expansions, illustrating how symbolic dynamics can encode arithmetic information. Special functions serve as a bridge linking these areas. Key examples include theta functions, hypergeometric series, and orthogonal polynomials, which reveal deep properties through their special values and transformation formulas. Recent advances in topological dynamics are also highlighted, including recurrence phenomena, limit sets in continuum theory, and the behavior of maps on regular curves and local dendrites. Analytic tools such as functional equations further illuminate the underlying arithmetic and geometric structures, providing a unifying framework across these disciplines.

  • Non-Kähler hyperbolicity for compact complex manifolds.
  • Bergman spaces and complex analysis.
